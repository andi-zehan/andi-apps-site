# Quick Meal Check Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 20, 2026

This draft privacy policy covers the Quick Meal Check iPhone app and the related support or marketing website.
It was written from the current codebase as inspected on April 20, 2026 and should be reviewed before production launch.

## 1. Overview

Quick Meal Check helps users estimate calories and macronutrients from meal photos or text descriptions.
To provide that service, the app uses account sign-in, cloud functions, subscription verification, and third-party AI processing.
We try to limit data collection to what is needed to run the app, support subscriptions, and improve reliability.

## 2. Information We Collect

### Account information

When you create an account or sign in, we collect an internal user identifier generated by our authentication provider and your email address if it is provided by Apple or the authentication provider.

### Meal submissions

When you analyze a meal, we collect the content you choose to submit, which may include:

- Meal photos you capture or select from your photo library
- Meal descriptions entered manually
- Optional notes such as ingredients, serving size, or corrections to the meal contents

### Analysis output and local meal history

The app can save nutrition estimates, timestamps, descriptions, and reduced-size preview and thumbnail copies of analyzed meal images locally on your device so recent entries remain available in the meal journal. In the current implementation, preview copies can be up to about 1600 px and thumbnails up to about 500 px on their longest side.

### Body metrics and goals

If you use the body metrics tools, the app stores information such as weight, height, age, sex, activity level, and calorie-goal preferences locally on your device.

### Subscription and service usage data

To unlock paid features and enforce service limits, we process subscription and account-service data such as product identifier, original transaction identifier, subscription expiration date, subscription update timestamps, and daily usage records including a per-day analysis count, last-analysis timestamp, and UTC day key. These records support the current limit of up to 50 meal analyses per account per UTC service day.

### Permissions

The app requests camera access for meal photo capture features and may request photo-library access to support selecting meal images. In the current implementation, these permission prompts can appear when the main capture flow is opened.

## 3. How We Use Information

- Authenticate users and keep them signed in
- Submit meal photos and text for nutrition analysis
- Return calorie and macro estimates in the app
- Save recent meal history and local settings on-device
- Verify subscription status and unlock paid features
- Enforce reasonable usage limits and protect the service from abuse
- Respond to support requests, security issues, and legal obligations

## 4. How Information Is Shared

We do not sell your personal information. We share data only as needed to run the service.

### Firebase and Google Cloud

We use Firebase and related Google Cloud services for authentication, callable backend functions, subscription-state storage, and usage records.
Backend infrastructure and service providers may also generate operational logs that include account identifiers or request metadata needed for debugging, reliability, abuse prevention, and service operations.

### OpenAI

Meal photos, meal descriptions, and notes submitted for analysis are sent through our backend to OpenAI so nutrition estimates can be generated.
If you include personal details in a meal note or description, those details may also be transmitted as part of the analysis request.

### Apple

Subscription purchases are processed by Apple.
We receive limited subscription-related data needed to determine whether paid features should be available in the app.

## 5. Data Retention

- Recent meal history saved by the app is stored locally on your device and is currently limited to roughly 30 days unless you delete entries sooner.
- Body-metrics preferences, calorie-goal settings, and onboarding state are stored locally on your device until you change them, clear the app, remove the app, or use the in-app account deletion flow.
- If you delete your account in the app, the app is designed to clear local meal history, saved body metrics, onboarding state, your backend user document, subscription records, usage records, and your Firebase Authentication account.
- Primary account-scoped backend records are retained only while needed to operate the service and are deleted when in-app account deletion succeeds. Service-provider logs, caches, and backups may still persist for a limited period under provider retention policies.
- Text-based meal submissions may appear in backend logs when needed for debugging, reliability, abuse prevention, or operations. Those logs can include account identifiers, request metadata, the submitted meal description, and the related analysis identifier.
- In the current app implementation reviewed for this policy, submitted meal photos are transmitted for processing but are not intentionally saved by us as a long-term user photo archive. Reduced-size preview and thumbnail copies may still be stored locally on your device for journal/history features. Service providers may keep transient logs or retain data under their own policies.

## 6. Your Choices

- You can choose not to grant camera or photo-library access, although some features will not work without those permissions.
- You can delete saved meal entries from the meal journal inside the app.
- You can delete your account from the Account section in the app.
- Deleting your account does not cancel App Store billing. If you subscribed through Apple, you must still cancel the subscription through Apple's subscription management.
- If you need help with privacy questions or the account-deletion flow, contact us at qmc-support@gmail.com.

## 7. Tracking, Advertising, and Sensitive Use

Quick Meal Check is not built around advertising or cross-app tracking.
We do not use the app to track you across other companies' apps or websites for targeted advertising.

Nutrition estimates can relate to health or wellness behavior, but Quick Meal Check is not a medical device and does not provide medical diagnosis, treatment, or emergency guidance.

## 8. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures intended to protect the information we process.
No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 9. Children

Quick Meal Check is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

## 10. International Processing

Depending on where you use the app, your information may be processed in countries other than your own, including countries where our service providers operate.

## 11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time.
If we make material changes, we will update the date at the top of the policy and may provide additional notice where appropriate.

## 12. Contact

For privacy questions, support requests, or help using the in-app account-deletion flow, contact qmc-support@gmail.com.
